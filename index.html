Global Indices

Asian equity markets closed mixed amid U.S. government shutdown fears. A shutdown may delay key economic data. Shanghai rose on stimulus hopes; Hong Kong rebounded. Japan fell as central bank members debated rate hikes. China’s PMI showed mixed signals, with private surveys exceeding expectations. European markets rose, despite lingering worries about Trump’s tariff threats and the risk of a U.S. government shutdown.

Investors weighed various regional economic reports and generally adopted a cautious approach. U.S. markets closed positive after a range bound session as market participants kept an eye on developments on Washington, where lawmakers are struggling to reach an agreement to avert a government shutdown.
